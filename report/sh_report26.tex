%                       Abstract  
%

\begin{abstract}
    Water ice is an incredibly complex condensed phase.
    The unique nature of its hydrogen-bonding network allows the formation of numerous different
    crystal and amorphous phases.
    In the past, molecular dynamics simulations of high pressure ices have had mixed results.
    This report introduces the use of a `polarizable' molecular potential model which could resolve some of 
    the issues that simpler models face.
    Stable anisotropic NPT MD simulations are done in LAMMPS at the phase boundary 
    of Ice--VII and Ice--VIII using the AMOEBA force field to test its suitability for high pressure conditions.
\end{abstract}
